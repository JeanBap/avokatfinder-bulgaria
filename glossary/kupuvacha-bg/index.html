Kupuvacha i Prodavacha (Buyer and Seller Legal Positions)

Understanding the distinct legal protections available to buyers and sellers under Bulgarian property law is essential, including disclosure obligations, defect liability, and the remedies available if the transaction fails.

Bulgarian property law allocates specific rights and obligations to both buyer and seller. The seller has a duty to disclose all known material defects (skrіti nedostatutsi — hidden defects) that are not apparent on inspection. Concealment of known defects gives the buyer the right to rescind the purchase or claim a price reduction.

Hidden defects claims in Bulgaria must be brought within 12 months of discovery for movable property, and there is ongoing debate about the applicable period for immovable property. In practice, defect disputes are most often resolved through court action or mediation, which can take years. Commissioning a technical survey (teknicheska ekspertiza) before purchase is a more reliable protection than relying on post-purchase defect claims.

The buyer's main obligation is to pay the agreed price on the agreed date. Failure to pay allows the seller to rescind and claim damages. Conversely, if the seller fails to deliver clear title, the buyer is entitled to rescission and return of all monies paid.

Unlike some countries, Bulgaria does not have a statutory 'cooling off' period for residential property purchases — once the notarial deed is signed, the transaction is complete and irrevocable (absent fraud, duress, or incapacity). There is no right to unwind a signed deed simply because the buyer changed their mind.

For foreign buyers, power of attorney arrangements mean the POA-holder's actions bind the buyer as if the buyer had acted personally. Ensure your POA is narrowly drafted to cover only the specific transaction and property identified — an overly broad POA creates risk of misuse.

Common Mistake

Watch out: Granting a broad power of attorney to an agent you do not know well is the single biggest risk for foreign buyers in Bulgaria. The agent can bind you to transactions you did not intend. Use only a licensed advokat (lawyer) with a narrowly specific POA.

Expert Tip

Pro tip: Commission a teknicheska ekspertiza (technical survey) from an independent structural engineer before the deed — not a survey arranged by the developer or the seller's agent. The survey cost is minimal compared to the potential liability from unknown defects.

Frequently Asked Questions

What are skríti nedostatutsi?

Hidden defects — defects that were present at the time of sale but not visible on reasonable inspection. The seller must disclose known hidden defects; failure to do so gives the buyer grounds for rescission or price reduction.

Can I get a refund if I find serious defects after purchase?

Potentially yes, but you must act quickly and prove the defects existed at the time of purchase and were not reasonably discoverable. This is a court process. Prevention (pre-purchase survey) is far preferable to cure.

Is a preliminary contract (predvаritelen dogovor) binding in Bulgaria?

Yes. A Bulgarian preliminary contract (predvaritelen dogovor) is legally binding — both parties can be compelled to complete the final notarial deed by court order, or the aggrieved party can claim damages if the other party refuses to proceed.
