Imotni Registar (Property Register)

Bulgaria's Imotni Registar is the national property register maintained by the Registration Agency, recording all ownership rights, mortgages, and encumbrances. A certified extract is mandatory before any purchase.

The Imotni Registar (Property Register) is Bulgaria's public record of all rights in immovable property, maintained by the Agentsia po Vpisvaniyata (Registration Agency) under the Ministry of Justice. Every property transfer, mortgage, easement, and court order relating to property must be submitted to the Registar to be enforceable against third parties.

Unlike many European systems, Bulgaria does not operate a strict 'intabulation principle' — ownership technically passes at the moment the notarial deed is signed, not at registration. However, without registration the transfer is not enforceable against third parties who deal with the property in good faith, making prompt registration essential.

The register is publicly searchable online via the Registration Agency portal. Buyers can obtain a certified extract (udostoverenie) listing all current entries for a property, identified by its unique identifier number or by the notarial deed description. The extract shows ownership history, active mortgages, annotations, and restrictions.

One important Bulgarian-specific risk: older properties in areas where the Cadastral Map has not been completed or updated may have discrepancies between the notarial deed description and the actual cadastral data. Always cross-check the Imotni Registar entry against the Kadastarlen Registar (Cadastral Register maintained by Agenciya po Geodeziya, Kartografiya i Kadastar — AGKK).

When multiple mortgages are registered against a property, their priority ranking is determined by date of registration — the earliest-registered mortgage has priority in enforcement. Buyers should ensure all pre-existing mortgages are discharged before or at completion.

Common Mistake

Watch out: Relying on the seller's verbal assurance that the property is 'free of encumbrances' is not due diligence in Bulgaria. Pull the certified Imotni Registar extract yourself — encumbrances not shown on the day of signing are not binding on the buyer, but only if you checked.

Expert Tip

Pro tip: Search the Imotni Registar by the seller's name (not just the property address) — annotations and restrictions entered against the seller personally can attach to the property if not identified and addressed before signing.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is an annotation (belezhka) in the Imotni Registar?

A note that a court action has been commenced affecting the property. An annotated property may be subject to a pending judgment that could affect title. Never buy an annotated property without understanding the underlying proceedings.

How do I find the property's unique identifier?

The unique identifier (edinstveno nomer) appears in previous notarial deeds and in the AGKK cadastral system. Your lawyer or the selling agent should be able to provide it.

Is the Imotni Registar the same as the cadastre?

No. The Imotni Registar records legal rights; the Kadastarlen Registar (cadastre, maintained by AGKK) records physical data — boundaries, areas, and building footprints. Both must be checked.
