What is Нотариален акт (Notarial Deed)?

An official document executed before a notary that provides legal proof of property transactions.

Key Facts — Нотариален акт (Notarial Deed)
  • Official document executed before a notary public
  • Provides authentic evidence of transactions and agreements
  • Required for certain transactions, including property transfers
  • Notary verifies parties' identity and authority
  • Recognized as evidence in legal proceedings

A Нотариален акт (Notarial Deed) is an official document in Bulgarian law that is executed before a notary public and provides authentic evidence of transactions, agreements, or declarations. Notarial deeds are particularly important in property law for transfers of ownership and other significant transactions.

Bulgarian law requires notarial deeds for certain transactions, including transfers of real property. The notary witnesses the execution, verifies the identity and authority of the parties, and maintains an official record. This authentication provides legal certainty and evidence of the transaction.

The Нотариален акт includes essential information about the transaction, such as the parties involved, property description, consideration, and any conditions or obligations. The deed is signed by the notary and parties, and copies are provided to all parties and maintained in official records.

Notarial deeds in Bulgaria are recognized as authentic evidence in legal proceedings. They provide proof of the transaction's date, parties, and terms. This authentication is valuable in property disputes and for establishing rights and obligations.

For property transactions in Bulgaria, notarial deeds are often required to ensure legal validity and to facilitate registration in the Property Register. The notary's involvement ensures compliance with legal formalities and provides a reliable record of the transaction.

Common Mistake: Assuming that informal agreements have the same legal effect as notarial deeds. For property transactions, notarial authentication is often required.
Expert Tip: Use notarial deeds for significant property transactions to ensure legal validity and obtain authentic evidence. The notary's involvement protects all parties.

Frequently Asked Questions

When is a notarial deed required in Bulgaria?

For property transfers and other significant transactions affecting property rights.

Can a notarial deed be modified after execution?

No, modifications require execution of a new deed or an official amendment.

What proof does a notarial deed provide?

It provides authentic evidence of the transaction, parties' identities, and transaction terms.
