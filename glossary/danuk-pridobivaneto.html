Данък Придобиване (Property Acquisition Tax)

Bulgaria's property transfer/acquisition tax — percentage-based tax on real estate transactions.

Overview

Данък Придобиване (Property Acquisition Tax) is Bulgaria's mandatory tax on the transfer of real estate ownership. The standard rate is 3-4% of the property's declared or assessed value, depending on whether it's an on-market sale, donation, or other transfer. For residential property, the standard rate is 3%. For non-residential property, certain transfers may be subject to 4%. The tax is typically paid by the buyer, though this may be negotiated in the purchase contract.

The tax base is the property's market value on the transfer date, or the declared value if higher. Tax authorities can challenge significantly undervalued declarations and assess properties at estimated market value. The National Revenue Agency conducts periodic reviews and comparisons to detect undervaluation schemes.

Exemptions and reduced rates are available for certain transfers. Transfers between spouses, direct descendants, ascendants, or adoptive parents and children may be exempt. Donations to charitable organizations and certain social transfers may also qualify for exemptions or preferential rates. Documentation proving the relationship or charitable status is required.

Payment is made to the Local Tax Office (местна ДГД) and proof of payment must be submitted to the Property Registry before registration can be completed. Unpaid acquisition tax prevents property registration, making the transaction invalid. Tax evasion through undervaluation is subject to penalties, fines, and potential criminal liability.

Recent changes in Bulgarian property tax policy have clarified valuation standards and strengthened enforcement. Professional property appraisals and comparable sales documentation help defend declared values against tax authority challenges.

Key Facts

  • 3% standard rate for residential property, 4% for non-residential
  • Calculated on property's market value or declared value (whichever is higher)
  • Typically paid by buyer; may be negotiated in purchase contract
  • Exemptions available for family transfers and charitable donations
  • Must be paid and documented before property registration

Common Mistake

Undervaluing property to minimize taxes — tax authorities actively review valuations and impose substantial penalties for undervaluation.

Pro Tip

Obtain a professional property appraisal (оценка) to support your declared value and defend against tax authority challenges.

Frequently Asked Questions

Are there reduced rates for property transfers within families?

Yes. Transfers between spouses, direct descendants/ascendants, or adoptive family relationships typically qualify for exemption or reduced rates (0-1%) with proper documentation of the relationship.

What happens if I don't pay the acquisition tax?

The property cannot be registered in the Gruntovnica. The transaction remains incomplete and legally invalid until the tax is paid. Unpaid taxes accumulate interest and penalties.

Is inheritance subject to the same acquisition tax?

No. Inherited property is subject to Наследствено право (inheritance tax), which has different rates based on the heir's relationship to the deceased. Direct descendants typically pay 0-1%, while more distant relatives pay higher rates.
