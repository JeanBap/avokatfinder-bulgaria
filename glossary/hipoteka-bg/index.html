Ипотека (Hipoteka)

Ипотека is the Bulgarian term for a mortgage — a security interest over real property registered in the Имотен Регистър, giving the lender enforceable rights against the property if the borrower defaults.

In Bulgaria, a ипотека (mortgage) is established by a notarial deed (ипотечен нотариален акт) and registered in the Имотен Регистър. Registration makes the ипотека effective against third parties — the lender holds a real right over the property in priority over unsecured creditors.

Bulgarian mortgage law distinguishes between договорна ипотека (contractual mortgage — granted voluntarily by the owner) and законна ипотека (statutory mortgage — arising automatically in certain circumstances, such as unpaid purchase price). Both require registration to be effective.

When buying Bulgarian property subject to an existing ипотека, the purchase contract should provide for the mortgage to be discharged from the proceeds at closing. The notary coordinates the simultaneous repayment and заличаване (cancellation) of the ипотека in the Имотен Регистър.

The Bulgarian National Bank regulates mortgage lenders. LTV ratios for residential mortgages are typically capped at 85% for primary residences and 70% for investment properties. Fixed-rate ипотека products are available but variable-rate loans linked to EURIBOR or SOFR are more common.

Common Mistake: Completing a Bulgarian property purchase without confirming that the seller's ипотека is cancelled at closing. An uncancelled ипотека remains registered against the property and is binding on any new owner.
Expert Tip: Structure the closing so the seller's ипотека is cancelled on the same day as the new notarialen akt is signed. Bulgarian notaries can coordinate this — ask explicitly for simultaneous заличаване and вписване.

After repayment of a Bulgarian ипотека, the lender provides a consent to cancellation (съгласие за заличаване). This is submitted with a заявление for заличаване to the Registry Agency — failure to file leaves the ипотека on the register despite the loan being repaid.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a заличаване in Bulgarian mortgage law?

Заличаване is the cancellation or deregistration of an ипотека from the Имотен Регистър after repayment of the underlying debt. The lender must provide a съгласие за заличаване (consent to cancel), which is then filed with the Registry Agency.

What is the difference between договорна and законна ипотека in Bulgaria?

Договорна ипотека is a voluntary contractual mortgage granted by the property owner to secure a loan. Законна ипотека is a statutory mortgage arising automatically by law — for example, where a property seller has not received full payment, a statutory lien arises in their favour.

Can a Bulgarian mortgage be enforced against a new owner?

Yes. A registered ипотека is a real right attached to the property, not to the person. If not discharged before a sale, it binds the new owner who acquires subject to the charge.
