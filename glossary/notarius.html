Notarius (Нотариус)

Bulgarian public notary authorized to authenticate documents, verify signatures, and certify property transfers.

Overview

A Notarius (Нотариус) is a Bulgarian legal professional authorized by the state to authenticate legal documents, certify signatures, and verify the identity and capacity of contracting parties. Notaries in Bulgaria are regulated by the Notaries Act (Закон за нотариусите) and are supervised by the District Court. They hold public office and their authenticated documents carry special legal evidentiary weight.

Bulgarian Notaries perform essential functions in property transactions: they verify party identity through official identification documents, ensure consent is freely given and informed, explain the legal consequences of the transaction, authenticate signatures, and prepare the Property Sale Contract (Договор за Продажба на Недвижимо Имущество). For real estate transactions, notarial authentication is mandatory and without it, the transaction cannot be registered with the Property Registry.

Beyond property transactions, Notaries authenticate wills (завещания), powers of attorney (пълномощни), marriage contracts, business agreements, and other significant legal documents. They maintain authenticated document archives and issue certified copies. All notarial acts are recorded in the official notarial registry and can be referenced by parties or third parties.

Becoming a Notarius requires a law degree, specialized training in notarial practice, and passing a state examination. Notaries must maintain professional liability insurance, maintain separate client trust accounts, and are subject to disciplinary measures by the Bar Association (Адвокатурата) and District Courts. Bulgaria sets standard fee schedules for notarial services, though some services permit negotiated fees.

The Bulgarian notarial system emphasizes prevention of fraud and provision of legal certainty. Notaries have a duty to refuse authentication if they suspect fraud, coercion, or legal invalidity. This gatekeeping role makes notarially authenticated documents highly trustworthy and greatly reduces disputes in real estate transactions.

Key Facts

  • State-authorized legal professional performing authentication and certification
  • Mandatory for property sale contracts and significant legal documents
  • Must verify identity, consent, and legal capacity of parties
  • Creates authentically signed documents with special legal weight
  • Regulated by Notaries Act (Закон за нотариусите)

Common Mistake

Confusing Notarius with Адвокат (lawyer) — Notaries authenticate; lawyers provide legal advice and representation.

Pro Tip

Request the Notarius to explain all transaction consequences and obligations before signing — this is their legal duty and ensures you fully understand the commitment.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is a Notarius required for all property sales in Bulgaria?

Yes. Bulgarian law mandates notarial authentication of all property sale contracts. Without notarization, the transaction cannot be registered with the Property Registry and has no legal effect.

Can I choose my Notarius?

Yes. Both buyer and seller can choose the Notarius, though sometimes one party may prefer a specific notary. If parties cannot agree, they may each choose their own Notarius to represent their interests.

What are typical Notarius fees for property sales?

Notarial fees follow a legal schedule based on property value. For residential property, fees typically range from 0.3-0.5% of the value, though this varies based on transaction type and complexity.
