Ipoteka (Mortgage / Hypothec)

An ipoteka in Bulgaria is a registered mortgage charge on real property, giving a lender the right to enforce against the property if the borrower defaults. It must be registered in the Imotni Registar to be valid.

The Bulgarian ipoteka (hypothec) is the standard legal instrument by which banks and other lenders take security over real property. To be valid against third parties, the ipoteka must be notarially documented (in a notarialen akt za ipoteka — mortgage deed) and registered in the Imotni Registar on the same day or shortly after the purchase deed.

When a buyer takes out a mortgage to finance a Bulgarian property, the bank typically requires the following sequence: purchase deed signed (transferring ownership); mortgage deed signed (creating the security); both deeds registered at the Imotni Registar on the same day. The bank releases the loan funds only after confirming the mortgage is registered.

Existing mortgages on a property being sold must be discharged before or at the time of sale. The seller's lender issues a zalichavane na ipoteka (mortgage discharge) document, which is submitted to the Imotni Registar to remove the charge. Buyers should confirm this discharge appears in the register before funds are released to the seller.

Mortgages in Bulgaria are available to Bulgarian nationals and to EU citizens. Non-EU citizens face more limited access to Bulgarian bank mortgages — some banks do not lend to non-EU buyers at all, though international banks operating in Bulgaria may offer alternatives.

Mortgage rates in Bulgaria have typically been among the lowest in the EU, though they rose with the ECB rate cycle from 2022. As Bulgaria moves toward Eurozone membership, rates and regulatory requirements are converging with EU norms.

Common Mistake

Watch out: Releasing purchase funds to the seller before the seller's existing mortgage is discharged from the Imotni Registar means you could acquire a property that is still encumbered. Confirm the discharge registration before any funds are released.

Expert Tip

Pro tip: Ask your mortgage bank for their specific sequencing requirements — the order of signing the purchase deed and mortgage deed, and how they confirm registration before releasing funds. Different banks have slightly different procedures.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I get a Bulgarian mortgage as a non-resident EU citizen?

Yes. Most Bulgarian banks offer mortgages to EU citizens who can evidence income, though the documentation requirements and LTV ratios may differ from those for Bulgarian residents. Prepare thorough income documentation.

What is the typical LTV for a Bulgarian mortgage?

Most Bulgarian banks offer up to 70–80% LTV for primary residential purchases. Lower LTV applies for secondary homes, investment properties, and rural locations.

How is the Bulgarian mortgage market changing with Eurozone accession?

Bulgaria is expected to join the Eurozone (adopting the euro) in 2025–2026. This will likely bring mortgage products and rates closer to ECB-linked standards and improve access for EU mortgage applicants.
