Имотен Регистър (Property Registry)

Bulgaria's Property Registry (Имотен регистър) — the national database recording all real estate ownership, mortgages, and encumbrances, maintained by the Registry Agency.

TL;DR Bulgaria's Property Registry (Имотен регистър) — the national database recording all real estate ownership, mortgages, and encumbrances, maintained by the Registry Agency.

The Имотен регистър (Property Registry) is Bulgaria's authoritative record of real estate rights, administered by the Агенция по вписванията (Registry Agency). Every notarially completed property transfer, mortgage, easement, and encumbrance must be registered here to be effective against third parties. Unlike some countries, registration in Bulgaria is completed by the notary on the day the deed is signed.

The Property Registry operates alongside the Кадастър (Cadastre), maintained by the Агенция по геодезия, картография и кадастър (АГКК). The cadastre records the physical characteristics of each parcel — area, boundaries, building footprint. The Property Registry records the legal rights attached to those parcels. Both must be checked before any property transaction.

Searching the Property Registry is possible online through the Registry Agency's portal. A search by property identification number (имот идентификатор) or owner name reveals current ownership, registered mortgages, easements, tax liens, and any lis pendens notices (вписана искова молба) indicating pending litigation. Official excerpts cost a small administrative fee.

In Bulgaria, a registered buyer generally has priority over an unregistered buyer, even if the unregistered transaction occurred earlier in time. This "first to register" principle means that once a purchase contract is signed, the buyer should complete registration as quickly as possible — ideally on the same day at the notary's office.

Recent legislative changes have been integrating the cadastre and property registry into a unified digital platform. Full integration is expected to significantly reduce the risk of inconsistencies between cadastral and registry records, which historically caused delays and legal challenges in property transactions.

⚠ Common Mistake: Relying solely on the seller's word that the property is free of encumbrances. Always obtain a certified Property Registry excerpt (удостоверение за тежести) dated within 24–48 hours of signing. Mortgages and liens can be registered at any time.
💡 Expert Tip: Request both a Property Registry excerpt and a cadastral reference plan (скица) before signing any preliminary agreement. Together they confirm ownership, boundaries, and the absence of encumbrances.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I obtain a Property Registry excerpt?

Apply online through the Агенция по вписванията portal or in person at any Registry Agency office. You need the property's имот идентификатор (property identification number) from the cadastral register. The excerpt (удостоверение за тежести) is typically issued within one business day.

What is the difference between the cadastre and property registry?

The cadastre (кадастър) records physical facts: parcel area, shape, building dimensions, land use. The property registry (имотен регистър) records legal rights: who owns the property, what mortgages exist, what court orders apply. Both are essential for due diligence — neither alone gives a complete picture.

Can a foreign buyer register property directly?

Yes. Foreign nationals can own and register property in Bulgaria on the same basis as Bulgarian citizens (with the exception of agricultural land, which requires special conditions for non-EU nationals). The notary handles registration on behalf of both parties on the day of signing.
