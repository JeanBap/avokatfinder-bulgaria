Notarius (Bulgarian Notary)

A notarius in Bulgaria is a state-licensed official who authenticates all property deeds. Without a notarial deed, no property transfer is legally valid. The notary also collects transfer taxes on the day of signing.

All Bulgarian real estate transfers must be executed before a licensed notarius (notary) within whose territory the property is located. The notary verifies the parties' identities, checks the Imotni Registar (Property Register) for encumbrances, reads the deed aloud, and collects the local property transfer tax (mestni danuk prihvarlane). The notarial deed (notarialen akt) is the only document that transfers legal ownership in Bulgaria.

Before the deed is prepared, the seller must provide a tax valuation certificate (danuchna otsenka) from the municipality. The higher of the agreed price or the danuchna otsenka forms the tax base for transfer tax. This protects municipalities from artificially low declared prices.

Notarial fees in Bulgaria are regulated by the Notarial Chamber (Notarialna Kamara) and are calculated on a sliding scale based on the property value. For a BGN 200,000 (approx. EUR 100,000) property, total notarial fees are typically BGN 500–900.

Foreign buyers who cannot attend in person may grant a notarial power of attorney (notarialno palnomoshtnо) to a Bulgarian lawyer. The POA must be apostilled if executed abroad and translated into Bulgarian by a certified translator.

After the notarial deed is signed, the notary submits it to the Imotni Registar (Property Register, maintained by the Registration Agency) for registration. Registration typically takes one to three working days.

Common Mistake

Watch out: Choosing the notary proposed by the developer or seller's agent means you share the notary with the other party. For a standard purchase this is often fine, but for complex transactions appoint your own notary.

Expert Tip

Pro tip: Confirm with the notary exactly which encumbrances appear on the Imotni Registar extract before the deed day. Any mortgage or lien on the property must be cleared at or before signing, with the seller's lender providing a discharge certificate.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need a lawyer if I have a notary?

The notary is a neutral official — they do not advise you on the merits of the transaction. A Bulgarian lawyer (advokat) reviews the contract, conducts due diligence, and represents your interests. Both are advisable.

What is the danuchna otsenka?

The municipality's own assessed value of the property, used as the tax floor for transfer tax. If your agreed purchase price is below this value, transfer tax is calculated on the higher danuchna otsenka figure.

Can I complete a Bulgarian purchase remotely?

Yes, via a notarially certified power of attorney granted to a Bulgarian lawyer. This is the standard route for foreign buyers who cannot be present in Bulgaria on the deed day.
