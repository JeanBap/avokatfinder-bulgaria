EGN (Единен Гражданский Номер)

Bulgaria's 10-digit unified civil number — assigned at birth; required for all legal transactions.

Overview

The EGN (Единен Гражданский Номер — Unified Civil Number) is Bulgaria's mandatory national identification number assigned to every citizen at birth. It consists of 10 digits: the first 6 digits represent the birth date in YYMMDD format, the next 3 digits identify the birth region/registration district, and the final digit is a check digit. The system has been in place since 1974 and is the primary identifier for all legal, tax, and administrative transactions in Bulgaria.

Each Bulgarian citizen receives their EGN upon registration with the local civil registry (ЗАГС — Registry Office). For non-citizens and stateless persons, an alternative Unified Identification Number (ЕДН) is issued. The EGN is printed on the Bulgarian national identity card (Национална Карта) and is required for employment, tax identification, healthcare access, banking, property ownership, marriage, and all government services.

The EGN integrates with Bulgaria's tax system (administered by the National Revenue Agency), social security systems, and the property registration system. When buying property in Bulgaria, both Bulgarian citizens and foreign buyers must ensure proper EGN or tax identification registration. The EGN is publicly disclosed in many official documents and registrations, making it less private than identification numbers in some other countries.

The check digit in the EGN is calculated using a weighted algorithm to validate the number's authenticity. Most government and financial systems automatically verify EGN validity before accepting it. Individuals cannot change or replace their EGN except in cases of documented error in the original registration.

Bulgaria's digital government services (E-Government Portal) increasingly use EGN for authentication and transaction authorization. Online property searches, tax filings, and business registrations often require EGN entry. Privacy is regulated under Bulgarian data protection law and GDPR, which restrict unauthorized collection and use of EGN information.

Key Facts

  • 10-digit national identification number assigned at birth
  • Format: YYMMDD + 3-digit region + check digit
  • Required for all legal, tax, administrative, and business transactions
  • Issued by local civil registry offices (ЗАГС)
  • Primary identifier for tax, healthcare, and property systems

Common Mistake

Treating EGN as highly confidential — while privacy-protected under GDPR, EGN appears in many public and official documents by law.

Pro Tip

Verify EGN validity using the official check digit algorithm before submitting to government or financial institutions to avoid processing errors.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can foreigners use EGN for property ownership in Bulgaria?

Foreigners do not have EGN numbers but are assigned tax identification numbers (ДДС/VAT or ЕИК/UIC for businesses) for property transactions and tax purposes. Bulgarian authorities require these for property registration.

Is EGN the same as the social security number?

Not exactly. EGN is the primary civilian identifier used across all sectors. There is also a separate social security number (БУЛСТАТ for businesses, personal social insurance number for employees), but EGN is the universal ID.

What if my EGN is lost or stolen?

Report the loss to your local civil registry (ЗАГС). You cannot change your EGN, but you can request a replacement identity card. The EGN itself remains valid and does not change.
