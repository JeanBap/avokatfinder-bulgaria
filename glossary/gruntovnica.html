Gruntovnica (Погребност/Поземлена Книга)

Bulgaria's property register (land book) maintained by local courts — official record of real estate ownership and encumbrances.

Overview

The Gruntovnica (Поземлена Книга/Land Book) is Bulgaria's official property register maintained by the Local Court (Районен Съд) for each municipality. It records the ownership, description, and legal status of all real estate including land, buildings, and apartments. The Gruntovnica is the authoritative source for property ownership and serves as proof of title. Registration in the Gruntovnica is mandatory and is the final step in completing a property transaction.

Each property is assigned a unique cadastral identification number (идентификатор на поземления участък) and appears in a specific property folio (лист за собственост) within the Gruntovnica. The folio includes owner information, property description, boundaries, area, value, encumbrances (mortgages, liens), and historical ownership records. Multiple owners or co-owners are recorded, along with their fractional interests.

The registration process begins when the notarially authenticated Property Sale Contract is submitted to the Local Court's Property Registry Department. After verification of the authenticated deed and payment of registration fees, the court registers the new owner. This registration creates legal presumption of ownership and protection against third-party claims. Unregistered property claims have limited legal force.

Mortgages, liens, easements, and other property burdens are recorded in the same Gruntovnica folio, providing transparent notice to potential buyers and creditors. Before purchasing property, buyers should request a current Property Registry Certificate (Удостоверение от Поземлена Книга) showing all encumbrances. This certificate demonstrates clear or burdened title.

The Property Registry is increasingly digitized, allowing online searches and electronic registration. Authorized persons (owners, lawyers, notaries) can access property information through the official portal. Registration fees are modest (typically BGN 50-100 equivalent for property transfers), making formal registration economically feasible for all transactions.

Key Facts

  • Official property register maintained by Local Courts for each municipality
  • Records ownership, description, boundaries, and encumbrances
  • Each property has unique cadastral identification number
  • Registration is mandatory and completes property transfer
  • Provides legal presumption of ownership and protection against claims

Common Mistake

Assuming property is transferred when the notarized contract is signed — legal transfer occurs only when registered in the Gruntovnica.

Pro Tip

Request a Property Registry Certificate (Удостоверение) before purchasing to verify current ownership, identify any liens or mortgages, and confirm the property description.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does registration in the Gruntovnica take?

Standard registration typically takes 3-5 business days after the authenticated contract is submitted. The court verifies documentation and registers the new owner upon completion. You can check status at the Local Court's property office.

Can I search the Gruntovnica online?

Yes. Bulgaria's Property Registry has an online portal where you can search by property address, cadastral number, or owner name. Detailed information requires proper authorization as an owner, lawyer, or notary.

What if there's an error in the Gruntovnica record?

Errors can be corrected through the Local Court's property office. Request a correction application with supporting documentation. The court will verify the error and issue a corrected registration entry.
