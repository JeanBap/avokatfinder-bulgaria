Usloviya Kartirane (Cadastral Registration Conditions)

Bulgaria's cadastral system (Kadastarlen Registar), maintained by AGKK, maps every property with precise coordinates and area data. Properties not yet entered in the completed cadastral map have less reliable boundary records.

The Agentsia po Geodeziya, Kartografiya i Kadastar (AGKK) maintains Bulgaria's digital cadastral map and register (Kadastralnat Karta i Kadastralnat Registar na Nedvizhimite Imoti — KKRI). Every property that has been surveyed and entered in the completed cadastral map has a unique parcel identifier (identifikator na imota) and precisely recorded boundaries and area.

However, Bulgaria's national cadastral mapping project is still incomplete in some areas — particularly older urban districts, certain rural areas, and zones with complex ownership histories. Properties in these uncompleted areas rely on descriptions in older notarial deeds, which are often imprecise and based on neighbouring landmarks rather than GPS coordinates.

For properties in the completed cadastral map, buyers should always request the cadastral certificate (kadastralnaizpis) from AGKK showing the parcel boundaries, area, and building footprint. This is separate from the Imotni Registar extract and must be reviewed independently.

A common Bulgarian due-diligence issue: the area stated in the notarial deed (from the 1990s or earlier) may differ from the area in the AGKK cadastral record. If the cadastral area is smaller, the effective property is smaller than you think. If larger, there may be unresolved boundary disputes with neighbours.

For new builds, the developer must submit the building to AGKK for cadastral registration before the apartments can be sold with individual identifiers. Delays in this registration can hold up title transfer to buyers.

Common Mistake

Watch out: Relying solely on the area stated in a 1990s Bulgarian notarial deed without checking the AGKK cadastral record is one of the most common sources of post-purchase disputes about property size and boundaries.

Expert Tip

Pro tip: Download the cadastral map extract for your intended purchase from kais.cadastre.bg using the parcel identifier. It takes minutes and visually confirms the property's boundaries and building footprint — essential for any plot or house purchase.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the kadastralnaizpis?

The official cadastral certificate issued by AGKK, showing the parcel identifier, area, boundaries, and building footprint for a property. It is the physical-data equivalent of the Imotni Registar extract.

Can I challenge the cadastral record if it shows the wrong area?

Yes. AGKK has a procedure for correcting cadastral errors. You need a licensed geodesist to survey the property and submit a correction request with supporting measurements.

For a flat in a block, is the cadastral area the same as the notarial deed area?

Not always. The cadastral area for an apartment includes the proportional share of common areas (corridors, stairwells) in some buildings, creating an apparent discrepancy with the deed's net area. Confirm the measurement basis with your surveyor.
