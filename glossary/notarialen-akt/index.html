Нотариален Акт (Notarialen Akt)

A notarialen akt is the Bulgarian notarial deed — the mandatory legal instrument used to transfer ownership of real property in Bulgaria, prepared and authenticated by a Bulgarian state notary.

The notarialen akt (notarial deed) is the cornerstone of every Bulgarian property transaction. Unlike many EU countries where a sale and purchase contract is separately certified, in Bulgaria the notary prepares the deed, verifies both parties' identities and legal capacity, and reads the deed aloud before signing. The deed itself constitutes the transfer instrument.

The notary charges for their services based on a regulated tariff linked to the property's declared or tax-assessed value (whichever is higher). Additional state fees include a local tax (местен данък) of 2–3% (set by the municipality) and a registration fee of 0.1% payable to the Registry Agency.

Once signed, the notarialen akt is submitted immediately to the Имотен Регистър (Property Register) by the notary. Registration is constitutive — legal ownership vests upon entry in the register, not upon signing.

Bulgarian notaries are assigned by the district of the property. Parties cannot freely choose any notary — they must use a notary with jurisdiction over the area where the property is located. Both parties must appear in person unless a power of attorney (пълномощно) is used.

Common Mistake: Declaring a purchase price below the tax-assessed value of the property. Bulgarian authorities assess local tax on whichever is higher — a low declared price triggers tax on the assessed value and may draw scrutiny for tax evasion.
Expert Tip: Obtain the данъчна оценка (tax assessment) from the local municipality before signing — it shows the minimum value for tax purposes and helps you calculate the total tax and fee burden accurately before completion.

For foreign buyers who cannot attend in person, a notarised and apostilled пълномощно (power of attorney) granted to a trusted representative enables the Bulgarian notary deed to be completed without the buyer's physical presence.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is a notary mandatory for Bulgarian property purchases?

Yes. Every transfer of Bulgarian real estate must be executed as a notarialen akt before a state-licensed notary with jurisdiction over the property's district. A contract alone, without the notarial deed, cannot be registered and does not transfer legal title.

What taxes are payable on a Bulgarian property purchase?

Buyers pay a local transfer tax (местен данък) of 2–3% of the higher of the purchase price or the tax assessment, plus a 0.1% registration fee to the Registry Agency, plus notarial fees calculated on a regulated tariff.

Can a Bulgarian property be purchased by power of attorney?

Yes. If the buyer cannot attend in person, a notarised and apostilled пълномощно (power of attorney) authorising a local representative to sign the notarialen akt is accepted by Bulgarian notaries. The PoA must be prepared with care to include the specific transaction details.
