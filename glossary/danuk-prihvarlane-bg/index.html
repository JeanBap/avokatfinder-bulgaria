Danuk Prihvarlane (Property Transfer Tax)

Bulgaria's property transfer tax (danuk prihvarlane) is a local municipal tax of 2–3% of the property value, collected by the notary on the day of the deed. New builds sold by VAT-registered developers may attract VAT instead.

When a Bulgarian property changes hands, danuk prihvarlane (municipal transfer tax) is levied at a rate set by each municipality within a statutory band of 0.1–3%. In practice, most Bulgarian municipalities apply rates of 2–2.5%. The tax is calculated on the higher of the agreed purchase price and the municipal tax assessment (danuchna otsenka).

The tax is collected by the notary at the time the deed is signed and remitted directly to the municipal treasury. It must be paid before the notarial deed is finalised — there is no option to defer it.

Separate from the transfer tax, a notarial recording fee (notarialna taksa za vpisване) is also due, payable to the Registration Agency. This is a fixed-percentage fee on the transaction value, typically around 0.1%.

Properties sold by VAT-registered developers — typically new builds — attract 20% VAT on the transaction instead of the municipal transfer tax. For high-value new builds, the VAT liability is significantly higher than the transfer tax it replaces. Buyers should always clarify with the developer which tax regime applies.

Non-residents who purchase Bulgarian property pay the same danuk prihvarlane as residents. Bulgaria has no additional non-resident surcharge on property purchases.

Key Facts

Tax baseHigher of agreed price or danuchna otsenka

Common Mistake

Watch out: Budgeting only for the 2% transfer tax without allowing for the notarial fee, registration fee, and legal fees means arriving at the notary short of funds. Total transaction costs in Bulgaria typically run 3–5% of the purchase price.

Expert Tip

Pro tip: Ask your lawyer for a full cost breakdown before the deed day — all taxes, fees, and disbursements — so you transfer the correct total amount. Arriving short on the deed day causes delays and potential rescheduling.

Frequently Asked Questions

Who pays the transfer tax — buyer or seller?

By law the buyer pays. This is non-negotiable — the notary collects it from the buyer before proceeding.

Can I negotiate the danuchna otsenka?

No. The municipal assessment is set administratively and cannot be negotiated. However, if you believe it is incorrect for your property, the municipality has a process to challenge the assessed value.

Do I pay VAT if I buy a new flat from a developer?

If the developer is VAT-registered (virtually all established developers are), the sale is subject to 20% VAT. This replaces the municipal transfer tax, but at a significantly higher rate. Factor this into your budget for new-build purchases.
