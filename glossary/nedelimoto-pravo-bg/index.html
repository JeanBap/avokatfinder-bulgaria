Нотариален Акт

A notarialen akt is a notarially authenticated deed in Bulgaria that formally transfers ownership of immovable property and must be registered in the Property Register maintained by the Registry Agency to create legal title against the world.

In Bulgaria, immovable property is transferred by means of a notarialen akt (notarial deed) authenticated by a notary public (notarius) competent for the area where the property is located. Unlike some EU jurisdictions where a notary may be selected freely, Bulgarian notaries have territorial jurisdiction — only notaries registered for the area of the property may authenticate its transfer.

The notary verifies the identities of the parties, confirms the seller's title by reviewing the property register, checks for encumbrances, and reads the deed aloud to the parties before authentication. Both parties must be present or represented by a notarially certified power of attorney (palnomoshno). The deed is then registered in the Imoten Registar (Property Register) of the Registry Agency.

Bulgarian law requires that the transaction value declared in the deed must not be lower than the tax valuation (danachna otsenka) set by the local municipality. If the agreed price is below the tax valuation, the notary must use the tax valuation as the declared value for stamp duty and notary fee calculation purposes.

The notary fee is calculated as a percentage of the declared transaction value, regulated by the Notary and Notarial Activity Act. Stamp duty (local transfer tax) is also calculated on the declared value. Both are typically borne by the buyer, though parties may agree otherwise. Buyers should request a full fee estimate before proceeding.

Once the deed is signed and submitted for registration, the Registry Agency issues an entry in the Property Register within three working days. Until registration is complete, the transfer is not enforceable against third parties. A buyer who does not register promptly risks being defeated by a creditor who registers a lien against the property first.

⚠ Common MistakeBuyers sometimes delay registering the notarial deed after signing, assuming ownership is secure. Until registration in the Property Register is complete, a creditor can register a claim against the property that takes priority over the unregistered buyer. Register immediately.
💡 Expert TipAsk the notary to file the registration application the same day the deed is signed. Modern Bulgarian notaries submit electronically and the Registry Agency processes registrations quickly. A same-day submission eliminates the window of risk between signing and registration.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I use any notary in Bulgaria for a property sale?

No. Bulgarian notaries have territorial competence. Only notaries registered for the district where the property is located may authenticate its transfer.

What is the danachna otsenka and why does it matter?

The danachna otsenka is the municipality's tax assessed value of the property. The declared value in the deed cannot be lower than this, as it forms the minimum basis for notary fees and transfer tax.

How long does the Registry Agency take to register a property transfer?

Standard registration takes up to 3 working days. Urgent registration (barzina registratsia) is available for an additional fee and is processed within the same working day.
